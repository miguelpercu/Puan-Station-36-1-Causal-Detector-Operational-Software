\subsection{Critical Configuration Points}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Electrical Authority Node:} The copper busbar defines absolute zero potential. All current returns must converge independently to eliminate voltage drops between devices.
    \item \textbf{Galvanic Isolation:} The ADuM3160 USB isolator is \textbf{mandatory}. It physically disconnects the PC's noisy power supply ground from the experiment's clean ground. Without it, high-frequency processor noise will inject into the INA128.
    \item \textbf{Floating Shield:} The shields of the 100 meters of twisted-pair cable connecting the 36 peripheral coils must be joined to the star point at \textbf{one end only} (preferably at the copper busbar). This forms a Faraday cage that carries no return current.
\end{itemize}

This topology guarantees that the \textit{Sobre Fuerza} (3.697) manifests on a pure signal, allowing the fixed gain of 1.9694 to operate exactly at the threshold of the Fifth Law.

\section{INA128 Instrumentation Amplifier: Precision Assembly}
The INA128 is an ultra-high-precision instrumentation amplifier, making it extremely sensitive. If the 51 k$\Omega$ gain resistor acts as an antenna, it will capture environmental EMI and corrupt the Bit 0 metric.

\subsection{Critical Connection Schematic (Central Node Shielding)}
To maintain the 3.697 torsion without distortion, follow this physical connection order:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Absolute Proximity:} The 51 k$\Omega$ resistor must be soldered as close as physically possible to pins 1 and 8 of the INA128. Every millimeter of excess wire is an antenna for ambient noise.
    \item \textbf{Reference Ground (Pin 5 -- REF):} The INA128 reference pin must run directly to the Copper Busbar via a dedicated wire. Do not share this return path with any other component. This is the ``anchor point'' of your signal.
    \item \textbf{Central Coil Shielding:}
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Use shielded twisted-pair cable to bring the central coil signal to the amplifier.
        \item \textbf{Golden Rule:} Connect the cable shield \textbf{only} at the copper busbar end. Leave the coil end of the shield floating (unconnected). This prevents the shield from carrying return current and becoming a noise source.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Step-by-Step INA128 Assembly Protocol}
\subsubsection{1. Chip Preparation}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Gain Resistor ($R_G$):} Solder the 51 k$\Omega$ (0.1\%) resistor directly across pins 1 and 8. Trim the excess leads flush with the solder joint. Do not use extension wires; the resistor must be ``mated'' to the chip body.
    \item \textbf{Decoupling Capacitors:} Place a 0.1 \textmu F ceramic capacitor between pin 7 (+V) and ground, and another between pin 4 (-V) and ground. This ensures the battery supply remains pure.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{2. Central Coil Connection (Signal Input)}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Input Pins:} Connect the two inner conductors of the shielded twisted-pair to pins 2 (In-) and 3 (In+).
    \item \textbf{Shield Management:}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textit{At the coil end:} Do not connect. Cut the shield braid and cover it with heat-shrink tubing.
        \item \textit{At the amplifier end:} Connect the shield \textbf{only} to the Copper Busbar (Star Ground).
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{3. Reference and Output (Anchoring to Bit 0)}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Pin 5 (REF):} This is the voltage reference pin. It must run via an independent wire directly to the Copper Busbar. This defines the ``Zero'' of your measurement.
    \item \textbf{Pin 6 (Vout):} The amplified signal exits here to your audio interface (Behringer UMC1820). Ensure the interface's ground also converges at the copper busbar.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{4. Power-Up and Verification Protocol}
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Voltage Check:} Before inserting the INA128, use a multimeter to verify that the batteries deliver a stable $\pm 12$ V (or $\pm 5$ V depending on your regulator).
    \item \textbf{Silence Test:} Connect everything but keep the 36 peripheral coils \textbf{powered off}. The monitoring software should show a background noise level near zero.
    \item \textbf{Phase Tuning:} Power on the 36 channels. If the star ground is perfect and the gain is set to 1.9694, the RMS will rise and stabilize in the vicinity of 0.7050.
\end{enumerate}
